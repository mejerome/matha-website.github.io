Jean Paul Ndjemba is Partner and Managing Director of Matha Capital Cote d’Ivoire. Until June 2017, he was Executive Director and Country Head of Global Banking for Standard Chartered Bank Cote d’Ivoire, overseeing Corporate, Investment and Institutional Banking, Treasury and Transaction Banking. Before relocating to Cote d’Ivoire, he held the position of Executive Director and Country Head of Corporate and Institutional Clients at Standard Chartered Bank Mauritius Limited from 2011 to 2014. Prior to that Jean Paul was Head of Global Corporate for Standard Chartered Bank Vietnam Limited, in addition to his Governance role as Chief Executive Officer of Standard Chartered Bank Hanoi Branch. Jean Paul was also the Chief Representative of Standard Chartered Bank in Vietnam’s Asia Commercial Bank, an affiliate company of Standard Chartered Bank between 2007 and 2009. In Singapore, he held the roles of Group Product Manager in Transaction Banking, overseeing Clearing and Continuous Linked Settlement in 56 countries and Senior Manager in Asia Governance and Strategic initiatives while at Standard Chartered Bank Singapore. He has a solid experience in many areas of Banking including Corporate and Investment Banking, Financial Markets, Transaction Banking and Governance. Jean Paul brings with him a wealth of diverse experience with 16 years in Banking across Asia and Africa. Jean Paul graduated from The George Washington University with degrees in Finance and Economics.

Co-partner of Matha Capital since January 2018, Aboubakary Guiro has more than 20 years of professional experience in the financial industry in Africa and Europe, in both Structured Finance and Banking Audit. From 2015 to 2017, Aboubakary has held the position of Director of Large Corporate and Commodities at Standard Chartered Bank Cote d'Ivoire and has been acting as the Head of Corporate Banking for 8 months. Prior Standard Chartered Bank, Aboubakary has held various roles over 16 years with BNP Paribas group including: Deputy Director of Corporate Clients at BICICI in Cote d'Ivoire, with the management of the Bank's participation in several Structured Finance projects in favour of the Ministry of Economy and Finance, FER and ANSUT, Business Manager at BICIGUI in Guinea Conakry, Senior Auditor at the BNPParibas General Inspection in France with several audit missions in the BNPParibas network in Africa, Europe and Asia. Graduate of the National School of Statistics and Applied Economics of Abidjan (ENSEA), Aboubakary began his professional career at the National Office of Technical Studies and Development (BNETD) as a Consultant in charge of studies in the Department Economic and Financial Studies. He has worked on studies related to the evaluation of the investment code as well as the competitiveness of the Ivorian economy.

Emmanuel Nambware is a banking and finance professional with 24 years experience mostly in Financial Markets. He has been Executive Director- Head of Rates and Credit Trading Emerging Markets for Standard Chartered Bank Ghana, with coverage of West Africa (excluding Nigeria) and selected Central African markets. Until recently, Emmanuel was also in charge of the Cote d’Ivoire Financial Markets. While Responsible for deploying Fixed Income and rates traded products in Ghana and the sub region, Emmanuel was instrumental in setting up the Ghana Fixed Income trading for the bank and the market at large. Having been a member of key market committees at the Ghana Stock Exchange’s Ghana Fixed Income Market, and a Vice President of the Financial Markets Association (ACI Ghana), Emmanuel has been involved in the development and deployment of trading instruments, government borrowing strategies and risk management solutions. Emmanuel joined Standard Chartered from Stanbic Bank in ZimbabwPe as Head of Asset and Liability Management and Fixed Income Trading and has covered such markets as Zambia, Nigeria and India in similar trading capacity. Before joining Standard Chartered bank, Emmanuel was Chief Dealer Money Markets at Stanbic Bank Zimbabwe. He holds a diploma in Treasury Management and Trade Finance and ACI dealing certificate.

Gilles Dockgne has 15 years experience in Financial Markets in Africa. He has been Head of Financial Markets Sales for West Africa for Standard Chartered Bank. He simultaneously ran the dealing room of Standard Chartered Bank in Cameroon. With teams in 04 countries (Cameroon, Cote d’Ivoire, Gambia and Sierra Leone) in the region, Gilles focused on servicing the institutional (sovereign, central and commercial banks) and corporate clients across Financial Market instruments including rates, foreign exchange, commodities and derivatives. Gilles Dockgne has actively participated to the arrangement, structuring, execution and implementation of foreign exchange risk management solutions through long term cross currency swaps, for the Republics of Senegal and Cameroon. Before joining Standard Chartered bank, Gilles was respectively internal auditor and Assistant Treasurer with Commercial Bank of Cameroon and Ecobank Cameroon. He holds a postgraduate diploma in Management of Banks and Financial Institutions from the Catholic university of Lille – France, and a Masters in Finance from the Skema Business school of Lille - France.

Christelle Mbakam has 8 years of banking experience at the International Bank of Cameroon for Savings and Credit (BICEC). Promoted at the Corporate Banking Department, Christelle was in charge of assisting the Key Account Managers, with the monitoring of commitments and the administrative management of client portfolios: from the opening of accounts to the notification of agreement or refusal of credit facilities, among other responsibilities. Thus, for many years, she had the privilege of following bilaterally, the financial indicators and business expansion of Cameroon's largest corporate and multinationals. Christelle Mbakam studied Administration and Marketing Management in Douala.
